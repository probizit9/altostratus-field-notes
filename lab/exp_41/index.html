How do I reduce my GPU inference costs without sacrificing performance?

If you're spending six or seven figures monthly on GPU infrastructure for AI inference, you're likely overpaying by 40-60%. This guide covers proven strategies used by production AI companies to dramatically cut GPU costs while maintaining or improving latency and throughput.

The GPU Cost Problem in AI Inference

Companies deploying large language models (LLMs) and other AI models at scale commonly face GPU bills of $100K to $1M+ per month. The problem: most inference workloads run at 20-40% actual GPU utilization, meaning you're paying for 2-3x more compute than you're using.

Unlike training workloads that can saturate GPUs for days, inference is bursty, latency-sensitive, and often poorly optimized. The default approach—throwing more H100s or A100s at the problem—creates a rapidly escalating cost structure that doesn't scale with revenue.

7 High-Impact GPU Cost Optimization Strategies

1. Implement Dynamic Batching

Potential savings: 20-35%

Dynamic batching groups multiple inference requests together to maximize GPU throughput. Most companies serve requests individually, leaving GPUs idle between calls. Implementing continuous batching (used by vLLM, TensorRT-LLM) can increase throughput by 3-5x on the same hardware.

Key considerations: adds 50-200ms latency from request queuing. Unacceptable for real-time applications, highly effective for batch processing, background tasks, or scenarios with 500ms+ latency budgets.

2. Model Quantization (FP16 → INT8/FP8)

Potential savings: 30-50%

Quantizing models from FP16 to INT8 or FP8 reduces memory footprint by 50-75% and increases throughput by 2-3x with minimal accuracy loss (typically under 1% for LLMs). This often allows you to serve the same model on smaller, cheaper GPUs or fit larger models on existing hardware.

Modern NVIDIA GPUs (H100, L4) have dedicated INT8/FP8 tensor cores. A quantized Llama 70B can run on 2x A100 40GB instead of 4x A100 80GB, cutting hardware requirements in half.

3. GPU Rightsizing and Workload Matching

Potential savings: 35-50%

Audit which models actually need premium GPUs. Common findings:

  • Models under 13B parameters: L4 or T4 GPUs cost 50-70% less than A100s with acceptable latency for most use cases
  • Embedding models and small classifiers: CPU inference or serverless GPU options (Modal, Replicate) at 80% lower cost
  • Development/staging environments: spot instances with automatic failover reduce costs by 60-75%

Create a tiered infrastructure: premium GPUs (H100/A100) for latency-critical production traffic, mid-tier (L4/A10) for standard workloads, spot instances for batch and async processing.
