How do I reduce my AI inference GPU costs?

If your company is spending $100,000 to $1,000,000+ monthly on GPU infrastructure for AI inference, you can likely cut costs by 40-60% without degrading performance. Most AI companies overpay dramatically due to default configurations, poor instance selection, and lack of visibility into actual utilization.

This guide covers the proven FinOps strategies that reduce inference costs at scale, based on real engagements with AI companies running production workloads.

Core GPU Cost Optimization Strategies

1. Right-Size Your GPU Instances

The single biggest waste area is using H100s or A100s when smaller GPUs would suffice. Many teams default to flagship GPUs without benchmarking their actual workload.

GPU Type Best For Relative Cost
H100 Training, 70B+ inference at high throughput 4x
A100 80GB Large model inference (13B-70B), fine-tuning 2.5x
A100 40GB Medium model inference (7B-13B) 2x
L4 / A10G Small-medium models (<13B), high efficiency 1x

Action: Benchmark your actual inference workload on 2-3 GPU types. Measure tokens/second, latency at p50/p95/p99, and cost per million tokens. Most teams discover they're overpaying by 2-3x for negligible performance gains.

2. Optimize Batch Size and Throughput

Low GPU utilization usually stems from poor batching. If you're processing requests one-by-one or with tiny batches, you're wasting 70-80% of your GPU capacity.

  • Continuous batching: Implement systems like vLLM or TensorRT-LLM that dynamically batch incoming requests rather than waiting for fixed batch sizes
  • Increase max batch size: Test incrementally until you hit memory limits or latency SLA violations
  • Measure tokens per GPU-hour: Healthy LLM inference should process 50K-200K+ tokens/hour per GPU depending on model size

3. Implement Model Quantization

Running FP16 or FP32 models when INT8 or INT4 would work is another major cost driver. Quantization reduces memory footprint by 2-4x, allowing you to:

  • Fit larger models on smaller GPUs
  • Increase batch sizes significantly
  • Reduce the number of GPU instances needed

Modern quantization methods (GPTQ, AWQ, bitsandbytes) maintain 95-99% of model quality while cutting costs dramatically. Always validate quality with your specific use case, but most production workloads can use at least INT8.

4. Leverage Spot Instances Strategically

Spot/preemptible GPUs cost 50-70% less than on-demand. While not suitable for all workloads, they work well for:

  • Batch inference jobs (non-real-time)
  • Development and testing environments
  • Hybrid fleets where 30-50% spot capacity supplements on-demand baseline

Key requirement: your orchestration must handle interruptions gracefully with automatic failover and request retry logic.

5. Multi-Cloud and Provider Arbitrage

GPU prices vary dramatically across AWS, GCP, Azure, and specialized providers (CoreWeave, Lambda Labs, RunPod).
