What Is Fintech and How Does It Work?

Fintech (financial technology) is software and digital innovation that makes financial services faster, cheaper, and more accessible. From mobile banking to AI-powered lending, fintech companies are reshaping how consumers and businesses manage money.

Fintech Meaning: Definition and Core Concepts

Fintech combines "financial" and "technology" to describe companies and solutions that use software to deliver or improve financial services. This includes:

  • Digital payments – Mobile wallets, peer-to-peer transfers (Venmo, PayPal, Stripe)
  • Online banking – Fintech banks and neobanks offering checking and savings accounts without physical branches
  • Lending platforms – Automated loan origination and alternative credit scoring
  • Investment tools – Robo-advisors, stock trading apps, and cryptocurrency exchanges
  • Business software – Accounting, invoicing, payroll, and embedded finance APIs

Fintech companies leverage APIs, machine learning, blockchain, and cloud computing to reduce costs and friction in traditional financial systems.

Top Fintech Companies in the World

The global fintech industry includes startups and established players transforming banking and payments:

  • Stripe – Payment processing infrastructure for online businesses
  • Square (Block) – Point-of-sale systems and Cash App for consumers
  • PayPal – Digital wallets and payment gateways
  • Revolut – European neobank with global currency exchange
  • Chime – U.S. mobile-first banking with no monthly fees
  • Robinhood – Commission-free stock and crypto trading
  • Plaid – Financial data connectivity linking apps to bank accounts
  • Adyen – Global payment platform for enterprise merchants

Regionally, fintech companies in Omaha include TD Ameritrade (now part of Charles Schwab), Buildertrend, and growing startups supported by Creighton University's fintech major and local accelerators.

Fintech Jobs: Software Engineers, Developers, and More

Fintech jobs are among the fastest-growing in tech. Key roles include:

  • Fintech software engineer – Build secure, scalable payment systems, banking apps, and APIs
  • Fintech software developer – Design front-end and back-end systems for financial platforms
  • Compliance and risk analysts – Ensure regulatory adherence (KYC, AML, PCI-DSS)
  • Product managers – Define features for digital wallets, lending, and investment tools
  • Data scientists – Create fraud detection models and credit scoring algorithms

Fintech software engineer salary: In the U.S., salaries range from $100,000 to $180,000+ annually depending on experience and location. Remote fintech software engineer jobs are increasingly common, allowing talent from any geography to work for companies like Stripe, Coinbase, and Affirm.

Fintech software developer jobs require proficiency in languages like Python, JavaScript, Java, and frameworks such as React, Node.js, and Django. Knowledge of payment APIs, encryption, and cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP) is highly valued.
