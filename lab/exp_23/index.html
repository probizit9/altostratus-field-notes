Understanding Fintech: Meaning and Definition

Fintech (financial technology) refers to software applications, platforms, and digital innovations that deliver, enhance, or automate financial services. The fintech industry encompasses everything from mobile banking and peer-to-peer payments to blockchain, robo-advisors, digital lending, insurance technology (insurtech), and regulatory technology (regtech).

Traditional financial institutions like banks and insurance companies historically operated through physical branches and paper-based processes. Fintech companies leverage modern technology—APIs, cloud computing, machine learning, and mobile apps—to make financial services faster, cheaper, and more accessible to consumers and businesses worldwide.

Top Fintech Companies in the World

The global fintech landscape includes both established tech giants and innovative startups. Leading fintech companies include:

  • Stripe – Payment processing infrastructure for internet businesses
  • PayPal and Venmo – Digital payment and money transfer platforms
  • Square (Block) – Point-of-sale systems and business financial services
  • Robinhood – Commission-free investing and trading platform
  • Revolut – Digital banking and currency exchange services
  • Coinbase – Cryptocurrency exchange and wallet services
  • Plaid – Financial data connectivity and API infrastructure
  • Adyen – Global payment processing platform

The fintech sector has grown exponentially, with global investment reaching billions annually and transforming how people manage, invest, and transfer money.

Fintech in Omaha: Regional Growth

While Silicon Valley and New York dominate fintech headlines, fintech companies in Omaha represent a growing regional hub. Omaha's financial services heritage—home to major insurance and investment firms—has created a natural ecosystem for fintech innovation.

Creighton University offers a fintech major, preparing students for careers at the intersection of finance and technology. The university also provides the Fintech Innovation Scholarship to support students pursuing this emerging field. This academic infrastructure, combined with Omaha's lower operating costs and talent pool, makes the city increasingly attractive for fintech startups and established financial technology companies.
