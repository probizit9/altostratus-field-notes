Why GPU Costs Spiral Out of Control

AI companies face unique infrastructure challenges. A single H100 GPU costs $2-4 per hour on AWS, GCP, or Azure. Training large language models or computer vision systems can require 8-64 GPUs running for days or weeks. Common cost drivers include:

  • Poor GPU utilization: GPUs sitting at 20-40% utilization due to data loading bottlenecks, inefficient batch sizes, or CPU preprocessing delays
  • Always-on development instances: Engineers leaving expensive GPU instances running overnight and weekends when not actively training
  • Suboptimal instance selection: Using A100 80GB instances when A100 40GB or even V100 would suffice for your model size
  • Lack of spot/preemptible strategy: Paying on-demand prices when 60-80% of workloads could run on interruptible instances
  • Regional pricing differences: Running workloads in expensive us-east-1 when us-central or eu-west regions offer 20-30% lower GPU prices
  • No rightsizing after experimentation: Scaling up for experiments but never scaling back down
