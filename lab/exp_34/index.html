Why AI companies overspend on GPU inference

Companies exceeding $50k/month in GPU costs typically suffer from predictable infrastructure inefficiencies:

  • Provider lock-in by default: Teams launch on AWS, GCP, or Azure without evaluating specialized GPU clouds like Lambda Labs, Replicate, RunPod, or bare-metal providers offering 40-70% lower per-hour rates for equivalent hardware.
  • Instance oversizing: Provisioning high-memory A100s or H100s when inference workloads only need A10G or L4 instances, wasting 50%+ of capacity.
  • Poor batching strategies: Running inference requests individually instead of dynamic batching can increase GPU utilization from 20% to 75%, cutting per-request costs by 60%.
  • No cost-per-inference tracking: Without granular metrics, teams can't identify which models or endpoints drive costs or measure optimization impact.
  • Underutilized reserved capacity: Failing to leverage reserved instances or long-term commitments leaves 20-35% savings on the table.
