How can AI companies reduce GPU inference costs?

If your AI company is spending more than $50,000 per month on GPU inference, you can likely reduce costs by 30-60% without sacrificing performance through systematic optimization, provider arbitrage, and infrastructure redesign.

GPU inference costs have become the dominant operational expense for AI companies at scale. Unlike training, which is time-bounded, inference runs continuously as your product grows—and costs scale linearly (or worse) with usage. Most engineering teams lack the specialized expertise to optimize this spending effectively while maintaining reliability and latency requirements.

The Four Pillars of GPU Cost Optimization

1. Workload Profiling and Right-Sizing

Most AI companies over-provision GPU resources because they lack visibility into actual utilization patterns. A systematic profiling engagement reveals:

  • Actual GPU memory usage vs. allocated capacity – Many workloads use only 40-60% of available VRAM, meaning you can serve the same models on smaller, cheaper GPU SKUs
  • Batch size optimization opportunities – Inference servers often run with suboptimal batch sizes, leaving GPU compute underutilized
  • Request patterns and queuing behavior – Understanding traffic patterns enables spot instance usage during off-peak hours and better autoscaling configurations
  • Model-specific performance characteristics – Different models have vastly different throughput on different GPU architectures; matching workloads to hardware yields 20-40% cost improvements

Right-sizing alone typically yields 10-25% cost reduction within the first month.

2. Multi-Cloud and Provider Arbitrage

The GPU cloud market is highly fragmented with dramatic price differences for equivalent performance. As of 2024:

  • AWS, GCP, and Azure charge $2-4/hour for H100 instances when available
  • GPU-specific clouds (CoreWeave, Lambda Labs, RunPod, Vast.ai) offer the same hardware for $1.20-2.50/hour
  • Spot and interruptible instances can be 60-80% cheaper but require orchestration expertise
  • Geographic arbitrage provides another 15-30% savings by routing workloads to regions with lower pricing

A well-designed multi-cloud strategy uses hyperscalers for baseline capacity (reliability, compliance, existing integrations) and GPU clouds for burst and batch workloads, reducing blended costs by 25-45%.

3. Infrastructure and Serving Optimization

The inference serving layer offers substantial optimization opportunities that most teams haven't explored:

  • Serving framework selection – vLLM, TensorRT-LLM, TGI, and Ray Serve have different performance profiles; choosing the right one for your workload improves throughput 30-80%
  • Continuous batching – Modern serving frameworks can dramatically increase GPU utilization through intelligent request batching, doubling effective throughput on the same hardware
  • KV cache optimization – PagedAttention and similar techniques reduce memory waste, allowing larger batch sizes or smaller GPU SKUs
  • Request routing and load balancing – Intelligent routing based on request complexity, model caching, and queue depth optimization prevents overprovisioning

Infrastructure optimization typically yields 20-35% cost reduction and also improves latency.

4. Model and Application-Level Optimization

The most impactful but underutilized optimization lever is the model itself:

  • Quantization – INT8 or FP8 quantization reduces memory footprint 2-4x, enabling smaller GPUs or higher throughput; well-implemented quantization maintains 95%+ quality
  • Model distillation – Smaller distilled models can handle 60-80% of requests at 1/4 the cost, with large models reserved for complex queries
  • Speculative decoding – Using a small draft model with a large verification model can reduce costs 40-60% for certain workload patterns
  • Prompt optimization and caching – Shorter prompts, cached embeddings, and semantic caching reduce redundant computation

Model optimization requires careful quality-cost tradeoffs but can yield 20-50% cost reduction when done correctly.
