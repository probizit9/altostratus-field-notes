How Do Local Services Ads Work and Are They Worth It for My Business?

Local Services Ads (LSAs) are Google's pay-per-lead advertising platform designed specifically for local service providers. Unlike traditional ads, you only pay when customers contact you directly—making them one of the most cost-effective advertising channels for plumbers, electricians, HVAC companies, locksmiths, lawyers, and other service professionals.

What Are Local Services Ads by Google?

Local Services Ads appear at the very top of Google search results—above traditional Google Ads and organic listings—when people search for local services. They display your business name, rating, phone number, service areas, and hours of operation with a prominent "Google Guaranteed" or "Google Screened" badge.

The key difference: LSAs use a pay-per-lead model instead of pay-per-click. You're only charged when a potential customer contacts you through the ad via phone call, message, or booking request. This makes budgeting more predictable and ensures you're paying for actual business opportunities, not just website visits.

Local Services Ads vs Traditional Google Ads: What's the Difference?

Feature Local Services Ads Traditional Google Ads
Pricing Model Pay-per-lead ($15-$150 per lead) Pay-per-click ($2-$200+ per click)
Ad Position Top of search results (above all ads) Below LSAs, above organic results
Verification Required Background checks, licenses, insurance No verification required
Trust Signals Google Guaranteed/Screened badge No built-in trust badges
Lead Quality Generally higher (direct contact intent) Varies (informational to transactional)
Control Level Limited (Google manages much of it) Full control over targeting, copy, landing pages

How to Set Up Local Services Ads: Step-by-Step

1. Create Your Local Services Account

Visit the Local Services Ads website or search "Local Services Ads login" to access the platform. Sign in with your Google account and select your business category and service area.

2. Complete Business Verification

You'll need to provide:

  • Business licenses: Current and valid for your service areas
  • Insurance documentation: General liability and any required professional insurance
  • Background checks: For you and anyone who interacts with customers
  • Business information: Name, address, hours, service areas

Verification typically takes 5-7 business days. Google is strict about these requirements to maintain the trust value of the Google Guaranteed badge.
